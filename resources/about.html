Ever since I was 2 years old, I've always been using a computer. There were a number of things that fascinated me, varying from the places available on the web to computer games to programs like Microsoft Paint. It's very likely that this influenced my interests today, which include gaming, drawing, and utilizing programs such as FL Studio or image-editing software. In addition, I've also made many programs on my TI-83 as well as practiced use of the Godot game engine.

After learning a substantial amount more of computer science concepts, it would be optimal to start looking into a career dedicated into the field. As a passion project, there are many creative ideas I have came up with, and eventually I would like to be able to implement them into games of their own. I have yet to determine what field of work I would go into, but software development is something I have been looking at.

My experience with programming is still developing, and so far I have completed one computer science course. However, there have been many side-projects I initiated during my time learning computer science concepts, and these have helped me in getting used to the process of planning, problem solving, and working. The rate at which I am able to understand things is fast, and I generally catch onto new concepts with ease.
